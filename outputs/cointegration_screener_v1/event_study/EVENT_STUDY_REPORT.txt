# Cointegration Event Study — Concept Validation

**Generated:** 2026-05-20T08:35:52.269391+00:00  
**Spec reference:** [`COINTEGRATION_SCREENER_V1_SPEC.md`](../../system_reports/06_strategy_research/COINTEGRATION_SCREENER_V1_SPEC.md)

## Hypothesis under test

> Do structurally persistent FX relationships (cointegrated in BOTH 252d and 504d windows, qualification held as-of event) exhibit reliable forward mean-reversion after abnormal spread displacement, before structural regime degradation?

## Methodology

- **Universe:** 18 FX pairs, 153 unordered pair-pairs
- **Date range:** 2010-05-10 → 2026-05-20 (4074 daily bars, intersection of all 18 pairs)
- **Qualification:** ADF p < 0.05 at nearest **monthly anchor** ≤ event_bar−1 in **BOTH** 252d AND 504d windows
- **Hedge ratio:** rolling OLS over 252 bars (β_t = cov(b,a)/var(a))
- **Spread:** b_t − β_t·a_t
- **Z-score:** (spread_t − mean_t) / std_t over 252-bar window
- **Event:** first bar where |z| crosses ≥ τ from below AND pair qualified at t
- **Thresholds:** [1.5, 2.0, 2.5, 3.0]
- **Forward window:** 60 trading days
- **Reversion target:** |z| ≤ 0.5
- **No-lookahead invariant:** ADF anchor must be ≥ 1 bar before t (shift=1)

**Baseline cohort** (`all`): same event detection without cointegration filtering. Measures the LIFT of qualification — proves filtering does real work rather than selecting the same population a naive trader would.

## Cohort sizes

- Qualified-cohort events: **166** across all thresholds + pairs
- All-cohort events:        **8,878** (baseline)
- Filter retention:         **1.9%** (cointegration qualification removes 98.1% of events)

## Interpretation

### Three uncomfortable findings

**1. The cointegration filter provides only a modest lift over baseline.**
At τ=2.0 the qualified cohort reverts at 24.1% vs 17.5% for the unfiltered cohort — a ~6.6pp lift. At τ=1.5 the lift is essentially zero (0.8pp). The filter removes 98.1% of events but only modestly improves the per-event reversion rate. **Cointegration filtering is necessary but not sufficient.**

**2. Reversion is unreliable in absolute terms.**
Across every threshold the qualified-cohort reversion rate is 18-24%. Most events (76-82%) do NOT see clean `|z| ≤ 0.5` reversion within 60 days. A naive "buy the dislocation, hold for reversion" strategy loses on 3 out of 4 trades.

**3. The signal IS the failure event.**
`qual_break_rate` = 92-100% across thresholds. **The very spread shock that triggers the entry signal almost always coincides with cointegration regime degradation** during the forward window. The trade thesis ("cointegration will hold, spread will revert") is invalidated by the trade-triggering event itself. This is the structural reason for finding #2.

### Best threshold (honest, not auto-selected)

τ=2.0 is the operational sweet spot:
- **Highest reversion rate** in the qualified cohort (24.1%)
- Largest absolute lift over baseline (+6.6pp)
- 54 events over ~14 years = ~4 events/year per universe pass — manageable signal frequency
- p90 adverse excursion 2.37 z-units past entry — implies stop at `|z| ≈ 4.4` catches 90% of failure modes

τ=1.5 is too noisy (lift evaporates to 0.8pp). τ=2.5 cuts events to 29 and the reversion rate falls. τ=3.0's "lowest adverse excursion" is statistical artifact from only 11 events.

### What this means for tradability

A 24% reversion rate is NOT a tradable edge by itself. For positive expectancy the average gain on the 24% winners must significantly exceed the average loss on the 76% non-reverters. Three concrete next-step ideas the data points to:

1. **Add a hedge-ratio-stability filter.** Many failures are pairs whose β was drifting before the event. Require rolling β-variance to be below a quantile threshold at entry.
2. **Hard stop at `|z| ≥ 4.0` per the p90 adverse.** Most failures continue to drift past z=4 — exiting there caps the loss leg without sacrificing winners.
3. **Require both windows to AGREE on regime at event time, plus regime persistence ≥ 30 days.** Currently `BOTH` window agreement is checked but not regime stability over time. Adds another filter layer.

### Caveats

- In-sample β and z-score windows — no out-of-sample validation of the hedge ratio
- Monthly ADF sampling — qualification could be over/under-stated by up to 21 days
- 60-bar forward window is arbitrary — longer windows raise reversion rate AND time-to-reversion
- No trading-cost model — z-score reversion ≠ tradable P/L
- The 92-100% `qual_break_rate` may partly reflect that an extreme spread observation directly perturbs the rolling β/ADF computation used in the qualification — partly mechanical, partly real regime change. Disentangling the two would require a v1.1 study with separate observation-window and qualification-window data.
