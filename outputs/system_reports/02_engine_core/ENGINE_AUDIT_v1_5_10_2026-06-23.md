## 0. Headline verdict

The weak point is exactly what was predicted: **error-handling philosophy, not arithmetic.** The engine's economic-realism assumptions are **clean** ‚Äî next-bar-open execution has no lookahead leak, gap-through-stop fills at the gapped open (realistic), and direction-aware spread is applied at every fill. What is fragile is the engine's **binary fail-fast model**: every contract violation either **crashes the whole run** or is **silently swallowed** ‚Äî there is no middle tier ("skip this trade and count it"). Three structural facts shape any fix:

1. **The invalid-stop crash is an `ECONOMIC_IMPOSSIBILITY`** (a gap/spread opens the fill past a valid strategy stop) **being treated as a fatal bug.** One such bar kills a 10-year directive. v1.5.10's spread-adjusted SELL fill (bid < ask) *widened* this exposure vs v1.5.8.
2. **The fill/stop logic is duplicated across up to 4 live sites** ‚Äî v1.5.10 *regressed* v1.5.9's shared-delegation pattern. Any fix must land in all live copies or single-asset and basket silently diverge.
3. **A typed exception taxonomy is inert until the downstream catch-all changes.** `run_stage1.py:1770` does `except Exception as e` and flattens every engine raise to a string. The taxonomy is a two-part change; the bridge is the load-bearing half.

**Net:** the audit *validates* the "amortize the ceremony" instinct ‚Äî there is a coherent Tier-1 cluster (skip-policy + de-duplication + taxonomy + the two cheap telemetry counters) worth one unfreeze cycle, and a clean bill of health on the parts people usually fear (lookahead, gap, spread).

## 3. The seam analysis ‚Äî how the C1 fix can (and cannot) be built

The operator proposed following the **warmup-wrapper pattern** (`main.run_engine` swaps `strategy.check_entry`/`check_exit` to enforce warmup *without touching the loop*, `main.py:88-113`). **This template does NOT reach the invalid-stop decision**, because warmup is decidable from `ctx.index` at **signal time (bar N)**, whereas fill-admissibility is only decidable from the **N+1 fill price inside the loop**. The `check_entry` wrapper runs one bar too early and never sees the fill.

Three real options, in order of fidelity vs. invasiveness:

1. **`check_entry` wrapper (closest to warmup, but blind to the fill).** Can reject a stop on the wrong side of the *signal-bar* price; **cannot** see a next-bar gap past the stop, and the engine-fallback stop doesn't exist yet at signal time. ‚Üí catches little of the actual trigger.
2. **Post-hoc flag-and-count in `run_engine` (no loop edit).** `run_engine` already owns the loop call; it can drop+count trades whose `entry_price` vs `initial_stop_price` violate admissibility, and emit a counter. Research-grade and zero-loop-edit, **but post-hoc** ‚Äî the trade already affected `session_trade_count`/one-per-day gating; you can suppress from output but not truly un-enter.
3. **Real loop patch at the existing `allow_direction` seam (`execution_loop.py:351-355`), positioned AFTER stop computation (`:390`).** This is the *only* place a true "skip the fill, stay flat, count it" can live ‚Äî and there is a structural precedent right there (`allow_direction` already does compute-then-skip-entry at this exact seam). Economically correct, but it forfeits the "don't touch the frozen loop" property and must be mirrored across the C2 copies.

**Recommendation:** option 3, **gated behind a directive opt-in flag** (`invalid_fill_policy: SKIP|FAIL`, default `FAIL` = byte-identical to today). This keeps every existing strategy's behaviour unchanged (freeze-intent preserved: default fail-fast stays the default), makes the skip explicit and counted, and ‚Äî because C2 de-duplication should land first ‚Äî is written once in the shared `build_position_from_pending()` rather than N times.

## 7. Scope ‚Äî TWO small patch releases, not one amortized cycle

The target is **split by blame boundary**: structural changes that must be *byte-identical* go in Patch A; the one change that *intentionally alters semantics* goes in Patch B. C2+telemetry are virtually guaranteed byte-identical; C1 is a deliberate behaviour change. Separating them gives cleaner blame and trivially easier rollback (if a result moves, it can only be Patch B).

**Ordering rationale (refined 2026-06-23):** `C2 ‚Üí H1+H3 ‚Üí C1 ‚Üí C3`. De-duplicate before touching invalid-stop behaviour (nothing should change while 4 copies exist). Then wire telemetry **before** the behaviour change, so the SKIP counts in C1 are measured against an established baseline ‚Äî otherwise post-C1 you can't tell whether `rejected_entries`/`stop_mutation_rejected` moved because of new behaviour or because the counter was just added.

### Patch A ‚Äî structural, byte-identical (promote ‚Üí freeze)
> **Scope LOCKED to MINIMUM CORE 2026-06-23** (investigation-engine criterion, omission-over-speculation). Patch A = six items only: **C2 shared builder ¬∑ health counters ¬∑ `engine_events.csv` ¬∑ minimal single-flag `invalid_fill_policy` plumbing ¬∑ `signal_version` bump (FAIL‚âÝSKIP) ¬∑ H6 fail-closed** ‚Äî all byte-identical. **Removed/deferred:** rejected_signals.csv ledger + SUPPRESSED layer, trade tags/context, trace mode, severity levels, reserved event types, schema versioning, capability blocks, generic `FLAG_SCHEMA`/`feature_set_hash` framework, new identity block. **Authoritative scope: [`ENGINE_PATCH_A_DESIGN_v1_5_11_2026-06-23.md`](ENGINE_PATCH_A_DESIGN_v1_5_11_2026-06-23.md).** *(Live fleet decoupled ‚Äî promotion is a pure research event; design doc ¬ß2.)*

### Patch B ‚Äî behavioural, intentional semantic change (promote ‚Üí freeze)
5. **C1 ‚Äî opt-in invalid-fill SKIP policy.** A trade whose actual N+1 fill is already on the wrong side of its stop (or has ATR‚â§0 / risk_distance‚â§0 / non-finite price) **never becomes a position** ‚Äî skip it and count it, instead of aborting the run. `invalid_fill_policy: FAIL|SKIP`, **default `FAIL`** (byte-identical to today; freeze-intent preserved). Written once in the Patch-A-centralized helper, at the `allow_direction` seam *after* stop computation. Emits `invalid_stop_skips`.
6. **C3 ‚Äî bridge support FIRST, full taxonomy LATER (incremental).** Taxonomy without bridge support is useless; bridge support *without* full taxonomy is already useful. So:
   - **C3 Phase 1 (this patch):** introduce ONE class, `TradeAdmissionFailure` (subclass `ValueError`, message strings preserved), raised at the stop/ATR/risk_distance/price-validity sites ‚Äî the "this trade never becomes a position" category. Change `run_stage1.py:1770` from a blanket `except Exception` to `except TradeAdmissionFailure: count + continue` (under `SKIP`) / re-raise (under `FAIL`), then `except Exception: abort`. This is the minimum that makes the SKIP policy expressible end-to-end.
   - **C3 Phase 2 (DEFERRED, own cycle):** the broader hierarchy (`StrategyContractError`, `IndicatorMissingError`, `EngineInvariantError`). The message-compat + test surface is larger than it looks ‚Äî not worth coupling to this release.

*Patch B acceptance:* byte-identical trades at `invalid_fill_policy=FAIL` (proves default unchanged) + positive test that `SKIP` drops exactly the known gap bars (2020-03-09 / 2022-06-01 / 2026-05-27) and increments `invalid_stop_skips` + convergence gate. Promote, re-freeze.
