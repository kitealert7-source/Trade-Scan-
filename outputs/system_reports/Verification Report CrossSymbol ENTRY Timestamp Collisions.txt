## Verification Report: Cross-Symbol ENTRY Timestamp Collisions

### Collision Frequency
- **37.5% of all entry events** (933 out of 2,490) occur at timestamps where ≥2 symbols fire simultaneously
- **416 timestamps** have multi-symbol collisions
- Breakdown: 332× two-symbol, 68× three-symbol, 15× four-symbol, 1× five-symbol collision
- Worst case: `2025-07-08 21:00:00` — 5 symbols fire simultaneously (AUDNZD, AUDUSD, EURUSD, GBPUSD, USDJPY)

---

### How the Bias Works

At any collision timestamp, the sort key is:
```
(timestamp, event_type_priority=1, trade_id)
```
Since all ENTRYs at the same timestamp share identical timestamp and event_type_priority, the **tiebreaker is `trade_id` lexicographic order** — which is the strategy prefix + symbol name. This produces a **permanent alphabetical processing order**:

```
1. AUDNZD  ← always first, always gets heat priority
2. AUDUSD
3. EURUSD
4. GBPNZD
5. GBPUSD
6. USDJPY  ← always last, always absorbs heat rejections
```

### Is This a Problem?

**For research/comparison:** No — the order is deterministic and reproducible across all runs. Results are consistent.

**For live deployment:** Yes — three real risks:

1. **USDJPY is systematically undertraded** relative to what a fair random-order simulation would produce. Its backtested PnL is understated.

2. **Portfolio metrics are order-dependent** — sharpe, drawdown, and correlation figures all reflect this alphabetical bias, not a neutral simulation.

3. **Live execution cannot replicate the order** — in live trading, broker fills arrive in unpredictable order. The live portfolio will have a different trade mix than the backtest at collision bars, making the capital simulation only an approximation.
